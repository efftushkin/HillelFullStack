The Samorost Series by Amanita Design

Explore the enchanting world of Samorost, a beloved series of point-and-click adventure games that have captivated players with their surreal visuals and imaginative puzzles.

Samorost 1

Released in 2003, Samorost 1 marked the beginning of Amanita Design's journey into the world of indie game development. This free Flash-based game introduced players to a charming gnome living on a small asteroid.

The story begins when a mysterious asteroid threatens to collide with the gnome's home. Players must help the protagonist navigate through a series of interconnected screens, solving intuitive puzzles to save his world. The game features no dialogue or text, relying entirely on visual storytelling and atmospheric sound design.

Despite its short length (approximately 15-20 minutes of gameplay), Samorost 1 established the distinctive artistic style that would become Amanita Design's trademark: hand-drawn graphics combined with photographic elements, creating a dreamlike, organic aesthetic. The game's success laid the foundation for the studio's future projects and demonstrated that innovative indie games could find an audience without massive budgets or marketing campaigns.

Key Features: Point-and-click gameplay, no text or dialogue, surreal art style, environmental puzzles, atmospheric soundtrack.

Samorost 2

Building on the success of its predecessor, Samorost 2 was released in 2005 as a commercial title. This sequel expanded the gameplay experience significantly, offering a longer adventure with more complex puzzles and environments.

The story follows the same gnome protagonist whose dog has been kidnapped by aliens. Players embark on an interplanetary journey, traveling through various bizarre and beautiful worlds to rescue their beloved pet. The game maintains the wordless narrative approach, allowing the visuals and sound to tell the story.

Samorost 2 refined the formula established by the first game, featuring improved graphics, more intricate puzzle design, and a longer playtime of approximately 1-2 hours. The game received critical acclaim for its artistic vision and creative puzzle mechanics. The soundtrack, composed by Tomáš Dvořák (Floex), became an integral part of the experience, perfectly complementing the surreal visuals with ambient electronic music.

Key Features: Extended gameplay, enhanced graphics, original soundtrack by Floex, more complex puzzles, interplanetary exploration, commercial release.

Samorost 3

After a decade-long wait, Samorost 3 was released in 2016, representing the culmination of Amanita Design's vision for the series. This ambitious sequel was crowdfunded through a successful Kickstarter campaign, demonstrating the strong fan support for the franchise.

Samorost 3 tells the story of a curious space gnome who uses the powers of a magic flute to travel across the cosmos, exploring nine unique worlds filled with strange creatures, alien civilizations, and cosmic mysteries. The game features the most expansive adventure in the series, with approximately 5-7 hours of gameplay.

The game showcases stunning hand-crafted visuals that blend photography, illustration, and animation into a cohesive artistic masterpiece. Each world has its own distinct character and ecosystem, populated by bizarre yet endearing creatures. The puzzle design is more sophisticated than ever, requiring players to observe their surroundings carefully and experiment with the environment. Once again, Floex provided an enchanting soundtrack that adapts dynamically to the player's actions.

Samorost 3 received widespread critical acclaim, winning numerous awards for its art direction, sound design, and overall creativity. It stands as a testament to what independent developers can achieve with passion, artistic vision, and dedication to their craft.

Key Features: Nine unique worlds, magic flute mechanic, dynamic soundtrack, 5-7 hours of gameplay, enhanced graphics and animations, Kickstarter-funded, multiple awards and critical acclaim.
